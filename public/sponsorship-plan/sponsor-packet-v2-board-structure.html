Why this rebalance exists

Make the sponsor ladder match the real work.

Packet v1 was a first pitch artifact. It has not been circulated as a promise to sponsors.

The v2 structure reconciles that draft with Haley's sponsor tracker, the May 7 development discussion, and the reality that meaningful sponsorship begins above casual support.

The goal is not more tiers for their own sake. The goal is a ladder where each level has a clear role, lower tiers are still valuable, and the top tier retains benefits that are genuinely special.

$1,000

North Star House · Sponsor Packet V201

Proposed structure

Five lanes, four formal sponsor tiers.

Friend of North Star

Working assumption

$150 or under $1,000

Supporter lane outside the formal sponsor ladder. Light recognition may be appropriate, but sponsor-package benefits remain an open approval question.

Heritage Partner

$1,000-$2,499

First formal sponsor tier. Real sponsor recognition and basic event access, without naming or lead-sponsor control perks.

Preservation Leader

$2,500-$4,999

Middle sponsor tier. Stronger recognition and more event access than Heritage, but still no event-level control.

Keystone

$5,000-$9,999

Major sponsor tier. Prominent recognition and limited association, filling the gap before Presenting.

Presenting Sponsor

$10,000+ or signature event

Lead sponsor tier. The only tier that should carry event-level control benefits, pending final approval.

North Star House · Sponsor Packet V202

Benefit logic

Separate recognition, access, association, and control.

Recognition

Public thanks, name/logo visibility, program listing, website recognition.

Access

Tickets, invitations, or sponsor attendance opportunities. Final counts remain bracketed.

Association

Sponsor name connected to a program, event, season, or visible body of work.

Control

Naming, remarks, press inclusion, banner placement, and event-level prominence.

Recommended rule

Lower tiers may receive recognition, access, and limited association. Presenting Sponsor is reserved for control benefits unless the board explicitly approves an exception.
